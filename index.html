Termez University of Economics and Service is proud to host an international student symposium titled "The World in 2050" a dynamic event bringing together young minds from across the globe to explore the challenges and opportunities that lie ahead.


As we move toward an increasingly interconnected and complex future, this symposium provides a platform for forward-thinking students to share research, exchange ideas, and inspire action on the issues that will transform our world over the next 25 years.


We invite students from all disciplines to join us in envisioning a better tomorrow, through critical dialogue, creative presentations, and global collaboration.

ABOUT US

Termez University of Economics and Service

An Academic Profile

Termez University of Economics and Service (TUES) is a dynamic and forward-looking higher education institution in Uzbekistan, strategically located in Termez, a city that historically functioned as a cultural and intellectual crossroads of Central Asia. As a private university dedicated to excellence in teaching, research, and community engagement, TUES is committed to preparing future leaders in economics, business, the social sciences, applied sciences, and professional service industries. Since its establishment, the university has strived to combine rigorous academic programs with practice-oriented training, while simultaneously cultivating a global vision through partnerships with distinguished institutions worldwide.


TUES now stands as one of the region’s most innovative educational institutions, recognized for integrating local and international perspectives in its curricula, advancing sustainable development practices, and providing its students with opportunities to participate in the international academic community. It is in this spirit of global intellectual exchange that the university hosts the international student symposium entitled “The World in 2050”, where students, academics, and professionals converge to explore challenges and opportunities shaping humanity’s shared future.
